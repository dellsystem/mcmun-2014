Mickaela Nixon

Secretary-General

Born and raised in Calgary, Canada, Mickaela proudly embodies Alberta's infamous diplomatic tact and understated sophistication. She has left the oil rich soils of Alberta for the culture capital that is Montreal and McGill, bringing with her a passion for Model UN. She is currently pursuing a degree in Physical Biology with a minor in Economics with the hopes of never being stuck in an office. In the rare instance when she is not studying, pipetting random fluids, worrying about McMUN or watching MASH she can be seen relaxing in her cowboy boots, talking about last night's hockey game.

Mickaela is excited to invite all Model UN enthusiasts to the beautiful city of Montreal, to enjoy the tastes, sights and sounds of her adopted home for what is sure to be a conference to remember.

Daniel Stysis

Chargé d'Affaires

Emma Stanton

Chief of Staff

Emma Stanton, known as “The Chief,” is in her fourth year at McGill, studying International Development Studies and minoring in Anthropology. Originally from Boston, Massachusetts, Emma has made her way up the eastern United States and Canada, living in Connecticut and Toronto, before calling Montreal home. While all the other kids were dealing with enraged hormones, Emma was participating in the Secondary School United Nations Symposium (SSUNS), Southern Ontario Model United Nations Assembly (SOMA), and The Hague International Model United Nations (THIMUN), as well. Emma knows that you’re no one until someone calls you bossy, so taking on logistical staff positions at SSUNS and McMUN, as well as Delegate Resource Center Coordinator at SSUNS 2011 and Page Coordinator at McMUN 2012 were nothing short of successes.

Emma’s not so guilty pleasure is her zest and fiery passion for Latin America and all things Spanish. Her pursuit of the quintessential Latin lover only comes second to her more philanthropic endeavors, being involved with the McGill chapter of Right to Play and serving as a member of the Arts Undergraduate Society Charity Committee. While Canada does not offer her the same warmth that Cuba, Mexico, or Colombia would, she has come to love lacing up her snow boats and throwing on her parka to enjoy Montreal’s amazing night life. Emma cannot wait to throw down at McParté and pub-crawl and provide the delegates of McMUN 2013 with an experience they will never forget!

Sabrina Ostrowski

Director of Public Relations

Born and raised right here in Montreal, Sabrina is McMUN’s Director of “Puuublic Relaaaaytions.” Polish at heart and Can’eh’dian by birth, she is a true citizen of the world. Her desire to become a super-human has led her to travel to almost thirty countries and take on six different languages, Arabic being her proudest feat…thus far. Sabrina studies International Management at McGill University, bearing minors in Middle-Eastern Studies and International Relations. To the untrained eye, the schedule she keeps is borderline insane, but her rebuttal lies in that her huge aspirations require quite the head start. She tells anyone who listens that she’ll one day stand as the Canadian ambassador to the IAEA, a fascination she fosters as the resident nuclear specialist of the McGill delegation. Oddly obsessed with Iranian politicians and an avid fan of the ukulele, she always finds time to explore Montreal’s finest breweries. Although Sabrina is the conference’s youngest Director of Public Relations yet, this will be her fourth McMUN, having previously participated as a delegate, Head Delegate, and Vice-Chair.

She will be taking her PR duties to unchartered territories and has made it her personal mission to keep the party (and diplomacy) going 24/7. Dubbed ‘the loud one’ of the Secretariat, Sabrina and her flamboyant blazers won’t be hard to miss during the conference.

Abhinav Gupta

Deputy USG-Committees, Crises

Born and raised in Montreal, Abhinav thought studying at McGill a natural choice since his primary schooling. Thinking he’d be studying science after his secondary schooling, he – and his unexpectedly old-school brown family – was taken aback by how speedily he decided to make the change to political science. Abhinav most probably thinks of political science as a great complementary to MUN. Only in his U2 in political science and having done six years of MUN, it wouldn’t be far off to call MUN more than just an extracurricular for him. It was love at first effort when Abhinav made a go at crisis committees for the first time. In keeping with that spirit, Abhinav wants all delegates in crisis committees at McMUN 2013 to experience what it feels like to be crisis virgins again. Given the extraordinary quality of delegates that attend McMUN, he realizes that this is going to be a tall order. Nonetheless, he feels like it’s one he can handle given that he’s 6’8” tall with a wingspan that would puzzle even Howard Hughes – we needed some family-friendly humour; take it or leave it. Abhinav takes advantage of this size by spanning the whole court when playing squash, having no choice but to have suits made to measure, and walking around with Shadi and Abdaal taking delegates’ lunches –especially if they call for the ever-loved clause-by-clause roll-call vote.

Lea El Houssami

Undersecretary-General, Finance

Envied by many, teased by some, Lea works at a zoo (who knew Montreal had zoos?). Her greatest ambition is to visit every country at least once and to wed Daniel Radcliffe – Lea would however argue that the latter is not a dream, but rather an inevitable occurrence waiting to happen.

This will be Lea’s third McMUN, assuming she will survive the months of balance statements leading up to it.
